# Why Build in Public

Very often, the most valuable part is not the final artifact. It is the long trail of revisions, failed ideas, and design trade-offs that got the work there.

That is why I want this blog to become a public workspace, not just a static showcase.

## What I want to capture

- why a system is structured the way it is
- why a certain approach failed
- what a deployment, refactor, or optimization actually fixed

## Why writing it down matters

There are two good reasons to keep this public record.

First, it helps me look back at the path behind a decision.  
Second, it helps other people understand what I am actually building, not just the polished result they see at the end.
